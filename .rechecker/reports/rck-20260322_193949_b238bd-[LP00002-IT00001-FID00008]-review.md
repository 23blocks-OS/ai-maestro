The old poll might still be running its `async` callback, and if that callback finishes *after* the new poll has started, it could potentially call `setResult` or `setError` with stale data, or even `clearPoll` on the newly established poll, leading to unexpected state updates or premature termination of the new poll.

For this specific case, the `clearPoll()` at the beginning of `handleBuild` combined with `pollRef.current = setInterval(...)` effectively replaces the old interval with a new one. The main issue is that the *previous* `setInterval`'s async callback might still be in flight.

1. Storing the `buildId` of the current build in state.
